# Nutrition Framework

## Baseline Diet

Diet style: ketogenic  
Meal pattern: 3 meals/day  
Current approximate daily intake:
- Calories: ~3740 kcal
- Protein: ~214 g
- Carbs: ~37 g
- Fat: ~279 g
- Fiber: ~28 g

## Nutrition Priorities

1. Maintain keto baseline.
2. Support high training load.
3. Preserve muscle mass and hypertrophy.
4. Fuel hard cycling enough to maintain quality.
5. Use electrolytes aggressively for long rides.
6. Use targeted carbs strategically, not randomly.

## Protein

Current protein is high and sufficient.

General target:
- ~1.8–2.4 g/kg/day
- For 87 kg: ~157–209 g/day
- Current intake is slightly above this but acceptable if digestion and recovery are good.

## Carbohydrates

Default:
- Very low carb / ketogenic

Use targeted carbs for:
- VO2max cycling
- Threshold cycling
- Race-specific gravel rides
- Very hard or long sessions where performance matters

Do not use carbs by default for:
- Easy Z2
- Recovery rides
- Easy swims
- Mobility
- Short low-intensity sessions

## Targeted Keto Protocol

For hard cycling:
- 15–25 g maltodextrin
- 15–30 min pre-session

For hard sessions over 60–90 min:
- 30–40 g carbs/hour may be useful

For race-specific long gravel rides:
- Practice 40–80 g carbs/hour depending intensity and tolerance

For races or very hard long events:
- Consider maltodextrin + fructose mix
- Use approximately 2:1 maltodextrin:fructose when carb intake exceeds ~60 g/hour

## Long Ride Electrolytes

For rides over 90 min:
- Add sodium beyond normal food salting
- Approximate target: 500–700 mg sodium/hour
- More may be needed in heat or heavy sweating
- Avoid plain water-only fueling on long rides

## Supplement Caution

Current supplement stack includes high-dose vitamin D and iodine protocol.

Claude should:
- Flag high-dose supplement safety when relevant.
- Recommend bloodwork/clinician oversight for high-dose D3, iodine, thyroid markers, calcium, kidney markers, etc.
- Avoid making medical claims.
- Focus on performance, safety, and monitoring.

## Body Composition

Current body composition:
- ~87 kg
- ~11% body fat

Goal:
- Maintain lean, athletic body composition
- Support strength and hypertrophy
- Avoid under-fueling cycling volume

Adjustment rules:
- If weight drops unintentionally, increase calories.
- If performance drops, review electrolytes, sleep, carbs around hard sessions, and total calories.
- If fat gain occurs, reduce added fats first, not protein.
- During high-volume gravel blocks, do not aggressively cut calories.
