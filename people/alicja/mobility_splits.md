# Alicja — Mobility & Splits

Flexibility is a **primary goal**. Mobility can be done daily; dedicated splits work 3–5×/week. **Never force a stretch into sharp pain** — work at a "strong but tolerable" stretch and breathe. Always stretch warm (after a session, after a hot shower, or after 5 min easy cardio).

## Daily mobility minimum (~8–10 min)

Hip flexors · hamstrings · adductors (inner thigh) · thoracic rotation · shoulders · ankles · deep-squat breathing hold.

## Splits routine (~15–20 min, 3–5×/week)

Do **after** a warm-up or training. Hold each 45–90 s, 2–3 rounds, easing deeper as you relax. Stretch both sides evenly.

### Front splits progression
| Stage | Drill | Cue |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Half-kneeling hip-flexor stretch | Tuck pelvis, tall torso |
| 2 | Low lunge → couch stretch | Rear knee bent, hips square |
| 3 | Hamstring stretch (seated/standing) | Long spine, hinge from hips |
| 4 | Supported front split (blocks/yoga blocks under hands) | Square hips, ease down |
| 5 | Front split (assisted → full) | Only as flexibility allows |

### Middle (side) splits progression
| Stage | Drill | Cue |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Adductor rock-backs (90/90 on floor) | Feet flat, sink hips back |
| 2 | Frog stretch | Knees wide, shins parallel |
| 3 | Cossack squat | Shift side to side, heel down |
| 4 | Wide-stance straddle stretch (seated/standing) | Hinge forward with flat back |
| 5 | Middle split (assisted → full) | Toes up, ease wider |

### Supporting mobility
- **Hips:** 90/90 transitions, pigeon.
- **Hamstrings:** active leg raises, jefferson curl (very light) for end-range strength.
- **Active flexibility:** add gentle PNF (contract–relax) and end-range holds once a position is comfortable — this builds usable, strong range, not just passive stretch.

## Progression rules

- Consistency beats intensity — short daily work outperforms occasional long sessions.
- Track depth with a simple landmark (e.g. "hands reach shins" → "palms flat") and note it in `workout_log.md`.
- Strengthen the end range, not just stretch it, to keep gains and protect joints.
