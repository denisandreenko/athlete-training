# Alicja — Running Plan (Has Aerobic Base)

Running is for **heart health**. She already has a base — comfortable running **4–5 km in Zone 2 without stops** — so she skips run/walk and starts by consolidating that base, then layers in structured intensity. Session-type definitions are in `shared/training_principles.md`. Fixed schedule: **2 runs/week — Tuesday (always easy) and Friday (easy in the base phase, the quality run once progressing).** Strength and mobility stay the priority.

## Phase 1 — Consolidate base + strides (weeks ~1–3)

- 2 easy runs/week, Zone 2, conversational. Keep one at her usual 4–5 km; let the other drift toward 6–7 km as it stays easy.
- Add **strides** to the end of one run: 4–6 × 15–20 s relaxed fast running, full walk recovery. Builds turnover with no real fatigue.

## Phase 2 — Introduce threshold (weeks ~4–7)

One quality run + one easy run per week.

- **Threshold run:** 10 min easy warm-up → 3–4 × 4 min "comfortably hard" / 2 min easy jog → 5–10 min cooldown. Build toward 4–5 × 5 min over the weeks.
- **Easy run:** Zone 2, 4–7 km.

## Phase 3 — Add VO2max (weeks ~8+)

Keep mostly one quality run/week; occasionally two when feeling fresh, never back-to-back hard days.

- **VO2max:** 10 min warm-up + strides → 5–6 × 1 min hard / 2 min easy → cooldown. Hill repeats (8–10 × 60–90 s uphill hard) are a good alternative.
- Alternate VO2max and threshold week to week; keep the second run easy.

## Rules

- ~80% of running stays easy, even with intervals in the mix.
- Easy runs are fine fully fat-adapted on keto — no special fueling. For genuinely hard interval sessions, a small pre-run carb dose is optional if energy feels low (see `shared/keto_nutrition_reference.md`).
- New niggle or sharp pain → drop the intensity and reassess; don't push through.
- Strength and mobility take priority — don't let a hard run compromise recovery for gym days (keep hard runs off the day before/after a leg-focused gym session).
