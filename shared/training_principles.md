# Training Principles (shared)

General methodology that applies to **both athletes**. Person-specific weekly structure, priorities, and loads live in each person's `training_plan.md` and `gym_training_plan.md`.

## Readiness & fatigue management

If a wearable is available (Denis — Garmin), use objective morning readiness. If not (Alicja), use the subjective fallback.

**Objective readiness (wearable):**

| Readiness | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 Green | HRV Balanced **and** Body Battery ≥ 60 **and** sleep score ≥ 70 | Train as planned |
| 🟡 Amber | HRV Unbalanced **or** Body Battery 40–59 **or** sleep 50–69 | Keep session, cut top-end volume ~20%, hold loads |
| 🔴 Red | HRV Low/Poor **or** Body Battery < 40 **or** sleep < 50, **or** 2+ amber days running | Easy/recovery only; apply removal hierarchy |

Objective markers override how you *feel* when they conflict — especially before hard intervals or heavy compound days.

**Subjective fallback (no wearable):** each morning rate sleep quality, soreness, energy, and motivation. If two or more are poor, treat the day as amber; if most are poor or sleep was clearly bad, treat it as red and apply the removal hierarchy.

**Physical warning signs:** poor sleep · elevated resting HR · persistent soreness · loss of motivation · declining performance · heavy legs for multiple days · irritability · reduced grip strength.

## Removal order when overloaded

Trim from the top down (least essential first). Each person's `training_plan.md` may specialize this, but the default order is:

1. Optional easy cardio (recovery run/spin/walk)
2. Extra skill or accessory volume
3. Main accessory volume
4. Hard interval volume
5. Long endurance duration
6. Main strength compounds (last resort)

## Deload

Every 4th week (or when fatigue markers accumulate):

- Reduce strength volume ~30–40% (drop a set per exercise or cut load ~20%).
- Reduce interval volume; keep one short intensity touch only if fresh.
- Keep movement quality and mobility; long/steady sessions stay easy.
- Prioritize extra sleep.

## Endurance session types

### VO2max / hill repeats
Improves top-end aerobic power and ability to repeat hard efforts.
Examples: 5 × 3 min hard / 3 min easy · 6 × 4 min hard / 4 min easy · 8–10 × 60–90 s hard uphill · 3 × 10 min of 30 s hard / 90 s moderate.

### Threshold / sweet spot
Improves sustainable pace and climbing/tempo power.
Examples: 3 × 12–15 min sweet spot · 4 × 8 min threshold · 2 × 20 min controlled threshold.

### Long / steady (Z1–Z2)
Aerobic durability and fat oxidation. Mostly easy, conversational. Fuel and hydrate per `keto_nutrition_reference.md`.

> **Beginner note (Alicja):** build an easy aerobic base with run/walk before adding any VO2max or threshold work. Keep most running easy; introduce structured intervals gradually and never more than ~1 hard run early on.

## Gym principles

- Preserve the main compounds; progress them by RPE — add load when all sets reach the top of the rep range at the target RPE.
- Accessories use double progression: add reps within range on all sets, then increase load.
- Keep most working sets at RPE 7–8 (novices: leave 2–3 reps in reserve and prioritize technique).
- Don't let leg fatigue from the gym blunt key endurance sessions — separate heavy leg days from hard cardio.
- Deload every 4th week.

## Calisthenics / skill work

- Frequent, low-fatigue, technically focused — **never to failure**.
- ~10–15 min per session, a few times per week, placed **before** heavy fatigue.
- Advance a ladder level only when the target is met with clean form (see `calisthenics_ladders.md`).
- Don't progress too many skills at once; pick a small set of primary skills and let the rest sit as tracked-only.

## Mobility

**Daily minimum:** hip flexors · couch stretch · thoracic extension/rotation · wrists · shoulders · deep-squat breathing.

**Post-session:** half-kneeling hip flexor 60 s/side · pigeon or 90/90 60 s/side · couch stretch 60 s/side · thoracic open book 10/side · cat-cow 10 cycles · wrist/shoulder prep before any hand-balancing.

## Do not do by default

- Don't add endurance volume just because capacity exists.
- Don't make every session hard.
- Don't turn skill or mobility practice into failure training.
- Don't stack hard legs, hard intervals, and a long/hard endurance session without recovery between.
